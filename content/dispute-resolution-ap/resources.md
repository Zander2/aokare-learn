## GAPS

The following areas had limited directly applicable articles; the resources selected are the best available but may require supplementary content:

1. **Lesson 2.4 (Rule Engine Architecture):** The IBM reference architecture page is technical and developer-oriented. A more business-friendly explainer on configurable AP rule engines (YAML/JSON-driven) does not appear to exist as a standalone public article from major AP vendors. Consider commissioning original content or using vendor documentation from Coupa or Basware.

2. **Lesson 3.3 (Cross-Vendor Anomalies):** No publicly available article specifically addresses coordinated multi-vendor anomaly detection in AP (e.g., simultaneous price increases across vendors). The Tipalti spend analysis article covers category-level monitoring but not network-level vendor coordination detection. This is an emerging research topic — consider referencing academic papers (e.g., BIS Working Paper on ML anomaly detection: https://www.bis.org/publ/work1188.pdf).

3. **Lesson 3.4 (Composite Scoring):** Most composite scoring literature is AR-focused or academic. The MindBridge article is the closest practitioner-level resource. Consider supplementing with the MDPI paper: https://www.mdpi.com/2674-1032/2/3/26 ("Examining the Plausible Applications of Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning in Accounts Payable Improvement").

4. **Lesson 4.2 (Supplier Dispute Messages — Localization):** No single article covers multi-language, multi-currency dispute message localization in AP specifically. The Gmelius template covers English-language basics only. Localization best practices for global AP communications may need to be drawn from general procurement localization guides.

5. **Lesson 5.1 (Human Decision Capture — Privacy):** The HITL resources do not address privacy and access control for AP decision data specifically (GDPR, data minimization for financial audit logs). Supplement with your organization's legal/compliance guidance or a CIPS data governance resource.

6. **Lesson 6.3 (Case Studies — Multi-ERP Global Enterprise):** No single freely available case study specifically addresses multi-currency, multi-ERP dispute resolution at global enterprise scale. The Medius/LUSH case study is the closest match for scale. For the multi-ERP angle, consider the Cognizant manufacturing case study: https://www.cognizant.com/us/en/case-studies/accounts-payable-invoice-automation.
